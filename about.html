Personal Journey

Ever since I can remember, I have had a passion for tecnology and how it all worked. Although I knew this, I also wanted to travel the world and mature as a person. This led me to sign up for the Royal Navy. I saw this as a substitute for going to University, a place where I could meet new people, gain leadership and problem solving skills under immense pressure.

So from the age of 20, I spent the next [ENTER YEARS] years in the Navy. My job title was 'Mine Warfare Specialist' which meant I looked for underwater explosives that could cause harm to people or international shipping routes. During this time, I travelled all through the medditerranean and the middle east, met incredible friends which I will have for life, got promoted to the rank of Leading hand and worked with state of the art robotic equipment designed to located and eliminate sea mines.
❮ ❯
Despite the positives that came from my military service, I knew I needed to prepare for when I left. This is when I discovered my love for coding. I was instantly hooked by the problem-solving and usefulness of programming. I started learning how to code with any spare time that I got; on weekends, after-hours and whilst on watch. I knew that this is what I wanted to do when I left the service.
